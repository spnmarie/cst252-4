Effects of Covid-19 on College Students' Mental Health

Group 4

  • Almost half of college students had a psychiatric disorder in the past year
  • Almost 1/3 of college students report they had trouble functioning because they felt so depressed
  • Mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, eating disorders and substance use are associated with lower GPA. This can lead to the higher probability of dropping out of college
  • 20% of female students have reported sexual assault or threatened sexual assault (according to the Center for Disease Control)
  • College directors of counseling services are telling us that there has been a steady increase in the number of students with severe psychological problems and that the demand for counseling services has grown at least 5 times faster than average student enrollment
  • Only 25% of students with a mental health problem seek help
  • Covid-19 has not helped
  • 1 in 5 of college students say their mental health has significantly worsened under COVID-19.
  • 80% of college students report that COVID-19 has negatively impacted their mental health
  • Younger adults are more likely to report symptoms of anxiety/depression during the pandemic
  • COVID-19 pandemic triggers 25% increase in prevalence of anxiety and depression worldwide
  • 71% of students indicate an increase in stress and anxiety due to COVID-19.
  • Over 40% of students report difficulties making friends and an increase of time spent indoors due to COVID-19.
  • Over a quarter of students experience financial difficulties because of COVID-19.
  • 52% of students experienced challenges in completing homework.
